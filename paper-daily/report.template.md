above exactly, replacing `{report_date}` with the target report date.

## Overview

- Summarize the day in 3-5 bullets.
- Mention the dominant themes across the selected papers.
- Call out any caveats about evidence, such as abstract-only reading.

## Top Papers

For each selected top paper, use this structure:

### {rank}. {paper_title}

- **Score:** {final_score}/5
- **Why selected:** Explain why this paper is worth attention for the research profile.
- **Motivation:** What problem or gap is the paper trying to address?
- **Core idea:** What is the main method, model, algorithm, or conceptual contribution?
- **Experiments:** What datasets, tasks, baselines, metrics, or empirical results are reported?
- **Strengths:** What looks convincing, reusable, or technically interesting?
- **Limitations:** What is unclear, weak, missing, risky, or only partially supported?
- **Relevance:** How this paper connects to the configured background topics and keywords.
- **Actionable idea:** One concrete experiment, implementation idea, or follow-up reading action.
- **Evidence level:** Use one of `abstract-only`, `paper page`, or `pdf`.
- **Link:** Include the paper URL.

## Potential Research Ideas
