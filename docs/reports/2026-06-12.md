### 1. NavWAM: A Navigation World Action Model for Goal-Conditioned Visual Navigation

- **Score:** 4.95/5
- **Evidence level:** pdf
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13494
- **Why selected:** Directly addresses the bottleneck between world model prediction and closed-loop action execution, highly relevant to VLN and spatial memory.

#### The Problem
Navigation world models predict future views but rely on external planners (like CEM) for action selection, creating a computational bottleneck and separating foresight from control.

#### The Solution
NavWAM is a diffusion-transformer policy that jointly predicts future egocentric views, goal-progress values, and action chunks in a shared latent sequence, turning visual foresight directly into executable closed-loop control.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Current egocentric view, goal image; outputs action chunks, future views, and goal-progress values.
- **Backbone or representation:** Cosmos Predict2 (diffusion-transformer) with a 9-frame latent canvas.
- **Core modules:** Joint denoising of visual and non-visual (state, action, value) latent frames.
- **Training or optimization:** Diffusion loss with upweighted action frames; simulation pretraining and real-robot adaptation.
- **Inference or deployment:** Receding-horizon execution without test-time CEM optimization.
- **Important design choices:** Encoding scalar values and actions as latent frames preserves the pretrained video-model interface.

#### Results
Evaluated on image-goal navigation. Outperforms planning-based NWM baselines and remains competitive with the 7B-parameter OmniVLA while using a 2B backbone and providing interpretable foresight.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Joint denoising of action and future views eliminates the need for test-time trajectory optimization.
- **Finding 2:** The goal-progress value frame effectively guides closed-loop behavior under partial observability.
- **Finding 3:** Visual foresight can be natively integrated into policy representations without sacrificing inference speed.

#### Strengths
Elegant unification of world modeling and policy learning. The latent canvas design is highly reusable for VLN and spatial memory tasks where future anticipation is required.

#### Limitations
Currently limited to image-goal navigation; language-conditioned goals and explicit 3D spatial memory integration are left for future work.

#### Relevance To My Research
A paper to read deeply and a method to reproduce. The shared latent sequence design is directly applicable to my work on memory design for vision-language navigation and world models.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Adapt the 9-frame latent canvas for VLN by replacing the goal-image frame with a language-conditioned spatial memory token sequence.

### 2. MoVerse: Real-Time Video World Modeling with Panoramic Gaussian Scaffold

- **Score:** 4.80/5
- **Evidence level:** abstract-only
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13376
- **Why selected:** Combines 3D Gaussians with video diffusion for real-time interactive world modeling, hitting multiple core priorities.

#### The Problem
Creating interactively navigable 3D scenes from a single narrow-FOV image is hard due to missing FOV and the need for persistent geometry and temporally coherent rendering.

#### The Solution
MoVerse separates world construction from rendering. It expands the input to a 360 panorama, lifts it into a persistent 3D Gaussian scaffold, and uses a Gaussian-conditioned video renderer for interactive roaming.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Single narrow-FOV image; outputs interactively navigable photorealistic video.
- **Backbone or representation:** 3D Gaussian scaffold, topology-aware diffusion, bidirectional diffusion teacher distilled into a causal autoregressive student.
- **Core modules:** Panoramic geometry-aware residual prediction, Gaussian-conditioned video rendering.
- **Training or optimization:** Distillation from a high-quality bidirectional teacher to a bounded-latency streaming student.
- **Inference or deployment:** Real-time scene roaming at 8 FPS on a single RTX 4090.
- **Important design choices:** Using explicit 3D Gaussians for long-range consistency and video diffusion for perceptual quality.

#### Results
Achieves 8 FPS real-time roaming on an RTX 4090, demonstrating a practical path toward single-image world creation with interactive video output.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Explicit 3D representations provide the necessary structural memory for long-range consistency in video generation.
- **Finding 2:** Distilling a bidirectional teacher into an autoregressive student enables bounded-latency streaming.
- **Finding 3:** Panoramic expansion prior to 3D lifting effectively closes the missing FOV gap.

#### Strengths
Directly addresses feed-forward 3D reconstruction, 3D Gaussian representations, and spatial memory. The separation of geometry and rendering is highly practical for deployment.

#### Limitations
Abstract-only evidence limits understanding of the exact geometric accuracy metrics and the robustness of the panoramic lifting in highly dynamic scenes.

#### Relevance To My Research
A paper to read deeply. The panoramic Gaussian scaffold is highly relevant to my work on feed-forward 3D reconstruction and spatial memory design.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Investigate the panoramic geometry-aware residual prediction module for initializing 3D Gaussians in our feed-forward reconstruction pipelines.

### 3. VISA: VLM-Guided Instance Semantic Auditing for 3D Occupancy World Models

- **Score:** 4.45/5
- **Evidence level:** abstract-only
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13460
- **Why selected:** Proposes a novel way to use VLMs to improve 3D occupancy world models for autonomous driving, directly aligning with my deployment priorities.

#### The Problem
Semantic 3D occupancy world models suffer from object and rare-class errors. Standard VLM alignment via crop-caption embeddings improves text-space similarity but fails to reliably improve closed-set occupancy mIoU.

#### The Solution
VISA uses VLMs as training-time semantic auditors. It queries an offline VLM on object crops, obtains a structured audit, propagates it along the object track, and distills it into semantic logits via reliability-weighted losses.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** 3D occupancy voxels, object tracks; outputs improved semantic logits.
- **Backbone or representation:** Existing occupancy world models (OccWorld, GaussianWorld).
- **Core modules:** Offline VLM querying, structured audit propagation, reliability-weighted taxonomy and scene-level audit graph losses.
- **Training or optimization:** Distillation of VLM audits into semantic logits during training.
- **Inference or deployment:** Inference remains unchanged and requires no VLM, ensuring deployment efficiency.
- **Important design choices:** Treating VLMs as reliability-aware auditors rather than generic embedding targets.

#### Results
On nuScenes, VISA improves OccWorld from 19.06 to 20.05 mIoU and GaussianWorld from 21.36 to 21.91 mIoU. Rare-class mIoU on GaussianWorld improves from 15.60 to 16.79.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** VLMs are better suited to closed-set occupancy as reliability-aware semantic auditors.
- **Finding 2:** Propagating structured audits along object tracks improves temporal state propagation.
- **Finding 3:** Reliability-weighted distillation effectively boosts rare-class performance without adding inference cost.

#### Strengths
Highly practical for autonomous driving deployment. The training-time auditing mechanism is a clever way to leverage large VLMs without compromising real-time inference.

#### Limitations
Requires offline VLM querying and robust object tracking, which may be computationally heavy during the data preprocessing phase.

#### Relevance To My Research
A method to reproduce and adapt. Directly applicable to my work on 3D world models and autonomous driving deployment.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Apply VISA's reliability-weighted distillation to our feed-forward 3D Gaussian/occupancy pipelines to handle rare classes and improve free-space interpretation.

### 4. From Imitation to Alignment: Human-Preference Flow Policies for Long-Horizon Sidewalk Navigation

- **Score:** 4.40/5
- **Evidence level:** pdf
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12603
- **Why selected:** Addresses the generalization-precision dilemma in long-horizon navigation with a lightweight monocular stack, highly relevant to robust robot deployment.

#### The Problem
Long-horizon sidewalk navigation with monocular RGB suffers from compounding errors, lack of social compliance, and a generalization-precision dilemma where diverse pretraining data compromises robot-specific control precision.

#### The Solution
FlowPilot uses anchored flow matching for policy pretraining to capture multimodal behaviors, followed by a human-in-the-loop preference learning scheme to align the policy with robot-specific precision and social compliance.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Monocular RGB, GPS goal; outputs future trajectory waypoints.
- **Backbone or representation:** FastViT encoder, anchored flow matching action representation.
- **Core modules:** Gated cross-attention to mitigate goal-driven shortcuts, preference alignment from human interventions.
- **Training or optimization:** Flow matching loss for pretraining; preference loss for alignment using corrective human actions.
- **Inference or deployment:** Mapless GPS-guided navigation on real-world sidewalk robots.
- **Important design choices:** Using discrete behavior priors (anchors) with continuous flow refinement to preserve multimodality.

#### Results
Achieves 42% success rate and 66% route completion in simulation. Real-world preference fine-tuning reduces intervention rate (IR) by 40.0% and non-intervention rate (NIR) errors by 52.1%.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Anchored flow matching effectively captures complex multimodal action distributions under long-horizon intentions.
- **Finding 2:** Gated attention mitigates attention-sink on the goal, encouraging better context utilization.
- **Finding 3:** Human-in-the-loop preference alignment successfully bridges the gap between large-scale imitation and robot-specific precision.

#### Strengths
Excellent focus on real-world deployment and social compliance. The lightweight perception stack (monocular RGB) makes it highly practical for micro-mobility robots.

#### Limitations
Relies on GPS for goal conditioning, which can be noisy or unavailable in certain urban canyons or indoor transitions.

#### Relevance To My Research
A method to reproduce for robust robot deployment. The anchored flow matching and preference alignment are highly transferable to our navigation policies.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Implement anchored flow matching for our local navigation policies to handle multimodal paths, and set up a teleoperation interface to collect preference data for alignment.

### 5. Foresight: Iterative Reasoning About Clues that Matter for Navigation

- **Score:** 4.00/5
- **Evidence level:** abstract-only
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12550
- **Why selected:** Proposes a test-time VLM reasoning loop for open-world navigation, highly relevant to VLN and spatial memory retrieval.

#### The Problem
Open-world mapless navigation from sparse language instructions requires resolving underspecified goals and inferring which environmental cues are relevant, which prior closed-set or open-loop methods struggle with.

#### The Solution
Foresight uses a finetuned VLM to alternate between proposing image-space motion plans and critiquing them using the language goal and visual context, guided by a reward model learned from human feedback.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** RGB, language goal; outputs refined motion plans.
- **Backbone or representation:** Finetuned VLM.
- **Core modules:** Plan-critique loop, RL post-training with a human-feedback reward model.
- **Training or optimization:** RL post-training to align plan critiques with open-set behavior preferences.
- **Inference or deployment:** Real-time test-time reasoning on a Jetson AGX Orin.
- **Important design choices:** Iterative motion refinement before execution, conditioning subsequent plans on prior critiques.

#### Results
Improves average task success by 37% and reduces interventions per mission by 52% relative to SOTA baselines across 6 real-world environments.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Iterative test-time reasoning with VLMs effectively discovers novel instruction-relevant cues.
- **Finding 2:** A plan-critique loop significantly reduces compounding errors in open-world navigation.
- **Finding 3:** RL post-training with human feedback aligns the VLM's critiques with deployment-specific preferences.

#### Strengths
Practical deployment on edge devices (Jetson AGX Orin). The plan-critique loop is a strong conceptual tool for VLN memory verification.

#### Limitations
Image-space plans might lack explicit 3D geometric grounding, potentially leading to collisions in complex 3D structures.

#### Relevance To My Research
A source of experimental ideas for VLN memory and planning. The critique loop can be adapted to verify spatial memory retrievals.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Integrate Foresight's plan-critique loop into our VLN spatial memory retrieval to verify if recalled landmarks and planned paths match the language goal before execution.

### 6. SPARC: Reliable Spatial Annotations from Robot Demonstrations at Scale

- **Score:** 3.90/5
- **Evidence level:** pdf
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13497
- **Why selected:** Introduces a physically grounded reliability score for 3D spatial annotations, useful for generating high-quality VLA training data.

#### The Problem
Automated spatial annotation for robot demonstrations lacks reliable quality signals; detector confidence is poorly calibrated for physical interaction, leading to noisy labels or discarded samples.

#### The Solution
SPARC leverages physical cues like phase-aligned motion and 3D gripper proximity to generate a composite reliability score for spatial annotations, reframing annotation as a selective-prediction problem.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Video, proprioception, language; outputs 3D object trajectories, start/target locations, and reliability scores.
- **Backbone or representation:** LLMDet for proposals, SAM2 for masks, AllTracker for dense tracking, MoGe-2 for 3D lifting.
- **Core modules:** Interaction-Aware Candidate Annotation, phase-aware motion scoring, robot-overlap filter.
- **Training or optimization:** Unsupervised scoring based on physical interaction evidence.
- **Inference or deployment:** Offline data processing pipeline to filter and retain high-quality trajectories for VLA training.
- **Important design choices:** Using physical interaction evidence rather than detector confidence for reliability scoring.

#### Results
On the 1.7k IA-Bench, SPARC achieves 80.2% interacted object localization accuracy vs 58.1% for detection-only baselines. Downstream VLA models finetuned on SPARC data achieve SOTA on object-grounding benchmarks.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Physical interaction evidence is a far better reliability signal than detector confidence in cluttered scenes.
- **Finding 2:** A continuous reliability score allows fine-grained control over the quality-coverage tradeoff without human review.
- **Finding 3:** High-quality automatic annotations match or exceed human-annotated data for embodied grounding.

#### Strengths
Excellent for generating large-scale 3D/trajectory datasets for robot learning. The 3D lifting and tracking pipeline is robust.

#### Limitations
Requires proprioception and specific grasp-phase heuristics, which may not generalize to all embodiments or non-prehensile manipulation.

#### Relevance To My Research
A related-work citation and useful tool for data generation. The 3D gripper proximity metric is a clever idea for interaction-aware perception.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Use SPARC's 3D gripper proximity and phase-aware motion scoring to filter our own robot demonstration datasets for VLA training, ensuring high-quality spatial grounding.

### 8. EA-WM: Event-Aware World Models with Task-Specification Grounding for Long-Horizon Manipulation

- **Score:** 3.60/5
- **Evidence level:** abstract-only
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13053
- **Why selected:** Bridges the gap between latent world model prediction and symbolic task progress verification.

#### The Problem
Visual or latent prediction in world models does not guarantee that task-relevant events (e.g., object moved, drawer opened) are satisfied, making long-horizon planning unreliable.

#### The Solution
EA-WM augments frozen visual-feature dynamics with task-specification-grounded event prediction and verification, scoring candidate futures based on task-progress and physical feasibility.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Visual features, task specifications; outputs structured event states and reliability scores.
- **Backbone or representation:** Frozen visual-feature dynamics model.
- **Core modules:** Event decoder, verifier that scores task-progress, semantic-consistency, and physical-feasibility.
- **Training or optimization:** Training the event decoder and verifier to align with task specifications.
- **Inference or deployment:** Guides sampling-based planning and gates candidate actions at test time.
- **Important design choices:** Using a verifier to score candidate futures in pretrained feature space.

#### Results
Evaluated on navigation, deformable-object, wall-constrained, and language-described manipulation (LIBERO wine-rack). Shows that event-aware verification makes feature-space world models more interpretable and aligned with task progress.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Event-aware verification effectively bridges latent prediction and symbolic task progress.
- **Finding 2:** Scoring candidate futures with physical-feasibility and uncertainty terms improves planning reliability.
- **Finding 3:** The verifier can successfully gate candidate actions in contact-sensitive settings.

#### Strengths
Provides a mechanism to make black-box latent world models interpretable and task-aligned. Highly relevant for long-horizon planning.

#### Limitations
Relies on frozen visual features, which may limit the model's ability to adapt to novel visual domains or highly dynamic scenes.

#### Relevance To My Research
Inspiration for world model planning. The event-verifier concept could be adapted to gate candidate actions in VLN or long-horizon robot navigation.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Consider adding a lightweight event-verifier head to our latent world models to score task progress and gate candidate actions during test-time planning.

- **Inference or deployment:** Applied to vision-based indoor positioning systems for stable motion estimates.
- **Important design choices:** Explicit error characterization rather than end-to-end black-box fusion.

#### Results
Data fusion improves localization accuracy over single-camera baselines. Measurement-calibrated fusion substantially reduces trajectory variance and improves motion smoothness, though absolute accuracy improvements over standard fusion are limited.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Explicit error characterization helps identify the true bottleneck in multi-camera pipelines.
- **Finding 2:** Calibrating fusion based on component-wise variance significantly improves motion smoothness.
- **Finding 3:** Black-box fusion obscures mechanistic failures that can be mitigated through targeted calibration.

#### Strengths

### 10. Mana: Dexterous Manipulation of Articulated Tools

- **Score:** 3.40/5
- **Evidence level:** pdf
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13677
- **Why selected:** Presents a highly effective sim-to-real framework for contact-rich dexterous manipulation, showcasing robust robot deployment.

#### The Problem
Dexterous manipulation of articulated tools is extremely difficult due to the need to coordinate internal degrees of freedom, maintain stable contact, and apply precise functional forces.

#### The Solution
Mana reinterprets dexterous manipulation as an animation problem, using a coarse-to-fine pipeline that transforms procedurally-generated grasp keyframes into manipulation trajectories via motion planning and RL.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Tool mesh, sparse functional affordance clicks; outputs manipulation trajectories and finger control commands.
- **Backbone or representation:** Point-cloud-conditioned transformer diffusion policy.
- **Core modules:** Grasp generator for keyframes, motion planning for reaching, RL for contact-rich transitions.
- **Training or optimization:** RL for short contact-rich phases, avoiding end-to-end exploration burden.
- **Inference or deployment:** Zero-shot sim-to-real transfer on an Allegro hand for 4 different articulated tools.
- **Important design choices:** Decomposing long-horizon manipulation into keyframes and short transition segments.

#### Results
Achieves zero-shot sim-to-real transfer for grasping and in-hand manipulation of tongs, pliers, clothespins, and syringes, demonstrating high success rates and precise force control (3-7N).

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Reinterpreting manipulation as an animation problem effectively bypasses the exploration difficulty of end-to-end RL.
- **Finding 2:** Decomposing tasks into keyframes and short contact-rich transitions preserves physical feasibility.
- **Finding 3:** Point-cloud-conditioned diffusion policies can generalize to small, thin tools with high precision.

#### Strengths
Impressive sim-to-real transfer for complex contact dynamics. The coarse-to-fine data generation pipeline is highly scalable.

#### Limitations
Requires manual affordance clicks (though minimal), and is specifically tailored to articulated tools rather than general rigid objects.

#### Relevance To My Research
Lower priority for 3D vision, but highly interesting for robust robot deployment and dexterous manipulation. The data generation pipeline is a strong conceptual tool.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Note the coarse-to-fine animation-inspired data generation for potential use in complex robotic arm navigation or interaction tasks where end-to-end RL fails.
