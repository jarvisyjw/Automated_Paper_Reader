### 1. AgenticNav: Zero-Shot Vision-and-Language Navigation as a Tool-Calling Harness

- **Score:** 4.85/5
- **Evidence level:** abstract-only
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10577

#### The Problem
Zero-shot VLN-CE relies on learned waypoint predictors that limit the action space and fail to leverage depth effectively, while memory handling often accumulates irrelevant context.

#### The Solution
AgenticNav rethinks zero-shot VLN-CE as an agentic interface, exposing action, depth, and memory as callable tools for VLMs, enabling direct pixel-level targeting and on-demand metric depth retrieval.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** RGB observations, depth maps, and language instructions map to executable motion commands.
- **Backbone or representation:** VLM backbone acting as an agentic harness.
- **Core modules:** Action tool (pixel selection), depth tool (on-demand metric distance), and memory tool (compact map image with recall).
- **Training or optimization:** Zero-shot, relying on the VLM's pre-trained tool-calling capabilities.
- **Inference or deployment:** The VLM selectively calls tools to navigate, avoiding long context windows.
- **Important design choices:** Replaces waypoint predictors with direct pixel-to-action mapping.

#### Results
Establishes SOTA on the R2R-CE benchmark among zero-shot methods. Real-world validation highlights strong zero-shot generalization compared to prior methods.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Direct pixel selection via the action tool outperforms traditional waypoint predictors.
- **Finding 2:** On-demand depth querying prevents the VLM from being overwhelmed by dense depth maps.
- **Finding 3:** Compact map memory with selective recall significantly improves long-horizon navigation.

#### Strengths
Highly relevant to VLN memory design and zero-shot robot navigation. The tool-calling paradigm is elegant and avoids heavy fine-tuning.

#### Limitations
Relies heavily on the underlying VLM's tool-calling latency and reliability, which might be a bottleneck for real-time control.

#### Relevance To My Research
Directly addresses my priority in memory design for vision-language navigation. A paper to read deeply and a source of experimental ideas for agentic VLN.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Implement the pixel-depth tool and compact map memory in our VLN pipeline to test if it reduces context-window overhead.

### 2. ManiSplat: Manipulation Trajectory Synthesis from Monocular Video via Decoupled 3D Gaussian Splatting

- **Score:** 4.65/5
- **Evidence level:** abstract-only
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10645

#### The Problem
Reconstructing dynamic and interactive 3D scenes with articulated robots from monocular video is difficult due to complex contact interactions and abrupt pose changes.

#### The Solution
ManiSplat introduces a unified framework that reconstructs controllable and decoupled Gaussian digital twins directly from monocular ego-view robotic videos using a Graph-Structured Disentangled Representation.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Monocular ego-view robotic videos to decoupled 3D Gaussian subfields.
- **Backbone or representation:** 3D Gaussian Splatting organized within a scene graph.
- **Core modules:** Graph-Structured Disentangled Representation, Task-Oriented Spatio-Temporal Alignment.
- **Training or optimization:** Joint photometric-geometric optimization using manipulation task logic (Motion vs Skill phases).
- **Inference or deployment:** Generates simulation-ready, temporally coherent digital twins.
- **Important design choices:** Separates robot, objects, and background into independently optimizable subfields.

#### Results
Demonstrates high fidelity and controllability for downstream robotic tasks and policy learning in interaction-driven dynamic scenes.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Decoupling Gaussians enables independent optimization of robot and object dynamics.
- **Finding 2:** Task logic stabilizes pseudo-ground-truth trajectories during optimization.
- **Finding 3:** Monocular 3DGS can be made simulation-ready with proper geometric constraints.

#### Strengths
Excellent for 3D Gaussian representations and robotic manipulation. Bridges the gap between static 3DGS and interactive robotics.

#### Limitations
Monocular input might struggle with severe occlusions during complex bimanual manipulation.

#### Relevance To My Research
Core to 3D Gaussian representation and feed-forward reconstruction. A method to reproduce for dynamic scene mapping.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Adapt the Graph-Structured Disentangled Representation for dynamic scene mapping and tracking in autonomous driving.

### 6. Rethinking Embodied Navigation via Relational Inductive Bias

- **Score:** 4.45/5
- **Evidence level:** pdf
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10348

#### The Problem
Open-vocabulary object navigation is frequently misled by false positives, outdated static priors, and repeated failed exploration due to a lack of embodied verification.

#### The Solution
DB-Nav reshapes the search space via dual relational biases: an Activation Bias (propagates contextual evidence) and an Inhibition Bias (suppresses unreliable regions).

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Visual observations and target categories to frontier exploration values.
- **Backbone or representation:** Relational Activation-Inhibition Exploration Graph.
- **Core modules:** Target-centric relational bias construction, multi-label object-role competition, bias propagation.
- **Training or optimization:** Zero-shot, relying on open-vocabulary detectors and predefined relational roles.
- **Inference or deployment:** Modulates frontier values online using observations and failed access events.
- **Important design choices:** Explicitly models 'what not to trust' via the Inhibition Bias.

#### Results
Significantly outperforms existing methods in Success Rate (SR) and SPL on ObjectNav benchmarks without costly online VLM reasoning.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Modeling negative inhibition is as critical as positive affinity for robust search.
- **Finding 2:** Multi-label competition stabilizes noisy open-vocabulary detections.
- **Finding 3:** Action-level falsification effectively prevents repeated visits to distractor regions.

#### Strengths
Lightweight, interpretable, and highly robust to noisy perception. Excellent for spatial memory design.

#### Limitations
Requires reliable open-vocabulary detectors and predefined object relation priors.

#### Relevance To My Research
Highly relevant to robot navigation and spatial memory. A paper to read deeply for frontier exploration.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Implement the Inhibition Bias in our semantic mapping module to suppress repeatedly failed frontiers.

### 7. AgniNav: Configuration-Driven Cross-Embodiment Local Planning for Robot Navigation

- **Score:** 4.00/5
- **Evidence level:** abstract-only
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10903

#### The Problem
Monocular local navigation policies are tightly coupled to specific robot embodiments, making cross-platform transfer difficult without retraining.

#### The Solution
AgniNav standardizes cross-embodiment transfer at the collision-envelope level using a measurable four-parameter safety envelope.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Monocular color images to 1D collision-relevant pseudo-laserscans and local plans.
- **Backbone or representation:** Height-conditioned image-to-scan network.
- **Core modules:** Image-to-scan predictor, dimension-aware local planner.
- **Training or optimization:** Trained on height-conditioned column-minimum scan labels from paired color-depth data.
- **Inference or deployment:** Runs at 30 Hz on Jetson Orin across wheeled, quadruped, and humanoid platforms.
- **Important design choices:** Decouples perception from planning via the 4-parameter safety envelope.

#### Results
Achieves high success rates (e.g., 39/40 on Turtlebot2) with near-zero collisions across diverse embodiments.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Standardizing at the collision-envelope level enables zero-retraining deployment.
- **Finding 2:** Pseudo-laserscans conditioned on height are sufficient for safe local navigation.
- **Finding 3:** The framework is highly compute-efficient for edge deployment.

#### Strengths
Highly practical for robust robot deployment and monocular navigation. Solves a real-world deployment bottleneck.

#### Limitations

#### Relevance To My Research

### 8. Diffusion Forcing Planner: History-Annealed Planning with Time-Dependent Guidance for Autonomous Driving

- **Score:** 3.80/5
- **Evidence level:** abstract-only
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11019

#### The Problem
Learning-based AD planners suffer from temporal inconsistency and tend to copy historical patterns instead of adapting to new environment contexts.

#### The Solution
Diffusion Forcing Planner (DFP) decomposes trajectories into history, current, and future segments, applying independent noise levels and history-guided control.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Historical and current states to future motion plans.
- **Backbone or representation:** Diffusion model with heterogeneous joint diffusion process.
- **Core modules:** Segment-wise noise assignment, classifier-free guidance (CFG) with annealed history.
- **Training or optimization:** Jointly denoises historical and future segments.
- **Inference or deployment:** Steers future sampling using annealed history in a controllable manner.
- **Important design choices:** Prevents history-copying by annealing the historical conditioning signal.

#### Results
Achieves competitive performance on nuPlan, producing continuous, stable, and controllable motion plans.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Heterogeneous noise levels prevent the model from overfitting to static history.
- **Finding 2:** Annealed history CFG improves adaptability to sudden environment changes.
- **Finding 3:** Temporal consistency is significantly improved over standard diffusion planners.

#### Strengths
Good for AD planning and temporal consistency. Addresses a known failure mode of diffusion planners.

#### Limitations
Diffusion inference latency might be high for real-time AD deployment without distillation.

#### Relevance To My Research
Relevant to AD deployment and planning. A method to reproduce for trajectory prediction.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Test the history-annealed CFG in our diffusion-based trajectory predictor to reduce temporal jitter.

### 10. Uncovering Vulnerability of Vision-Language-Action Models under Joint-Level Physical Faults

- **Score:** 3.80/5
- **Evidence level:** abstract-only
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10501

#### The Problem
VLA models are vulnerable to joint-level physical faults (e.g., friction, degradation) that disrupt the learned closed-loop relationship between actions and observations.

#### The Solution
J-PARC is a lightweight residual calibration framework that infers latent joint-fault regimes and conditions a shared residual calibrator for adaptive action correction.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** VLA predicted actions and recent joint dynamics to calibrated actions.
- **Backbone or representation:** Frozen VLA policy with a residual calibrator head.
- **Core modules:** Latent joint-fault regime inference, shared residual calibrator.
- **Training or optimization:** Trained to minimize execution mismatch under simulated physical faults.
- **Inference or deployment:** Runs online alongside the frozen VLA policy.
- **Important design choices:** Adapts to feasible faults like friction without requiring full policy retraining.

#### Results
Improves robustness under joint-level faults while preserving fault-free environment performance.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Feasible faults like increased friction still cause severe closed-loop execution mismatch.
- **Finding 2:** Latent regime inference allows a single calibrator to handle diverse fault types.
- **Finding 3:** Residual calibration is sufficient to recover VLA performance without retraining.

#### Strengths
Highly relevant to robust robot deployment and calibration. Addresses a critical real-world failure mode.

#### Limitations
Requires online joint dynamic monitoring, which might be noisy on low-cost hardware.

#### Relevance To My Research
