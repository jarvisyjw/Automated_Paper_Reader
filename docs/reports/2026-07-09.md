### 1. GeoGS-SLAM: Geometry-Only Gaussian Splatting for Dense Monocular SLAM

- **Score:** 5.0/5
- **Evidence level:** `pdf`
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07452

#### The Problem
Dense visual SLAM using 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) typically models both appearance and geometry. However, downstream robotic tasks like navigation and obstacle avoidance rely primarily on accurate spatial geometry. Appearance modeling adds significant computational overhead and is highly sensitive to illumination variations, which hinders robust deployment.

#### The Solution
The paper proposes GeoGS-SLAM, a dense monocular SLAM system built upon Geometry-only Gaussian Splatting (GeoGS). By dropping appearance parameters, it focuses purely on spatial geometry, accelerating convergence and improving robustness. It also introduces a unified Sim(3) map update strategy for loop closure to prevent map tearing.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Monocular RGB/depth streams $\rightarrow$ dense geometric 3D map and camera poses.
- **Backbone or representation:** Geometry-only 3D Gaussians, retaining only spatial parameters and reducing per-primitive parameters by over 80%.
- **Core modules:** Local-plane driven initialization, single/multi-view geometric and photometric supervision, and a Sim(3) global map update module.
- **Training or optimization:** Optimizes Gaussian primitives via geometric and photometric losses without optimizing spherical harmonics or color.
- **Inference or deployment:** Online mapping and tracking, highly efficient due to the reduced parameter space.
- **Important design choices:** Uses Sim(3) global alignment for loop closure rather than per-viewpoint pose corrections, which prevents map tearing and inconsistency.

#### Results
Extensive experiments on synthetic and real-world benchmarks demonstrate that GeoGS-SLAM outperforms state-of-the-art methods in online mapping efficiency and geometric reconstruction quality, while showing enhanced robustness to illumination changes.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Dropping appearance parameters significantly accelerates geometric convergence and reduces memory footprint.
- **Finding 2:** A unified Sim(3) map update strategy effectively prevents map tearing during loop closure in dense Gaussian maps.
- **Finding 3:** Geometry-only 3DGS is inherently more robust to illumination variations than appearance-coupled 3DGS.

#### Strengths
Highly efficient and directly applicable to robotics where geometric accuracy outweighs photorealism. The loop closure map update is a strong, practical contribution that solves a known pain point in dense SLAM.

#### Limitations
Dropping appearance entirely might limit the map's utility for tasks requiring visual place recognition, texture-based semantic understanding, or human-in-the-loop teleoperation where visual feedback is necessary.

#### Relevance To My Research
Directly aligns with core interests in 3DGS, SLAM, and loop closure. This is a **paper to read deeply** and potentially reproduce for geometric mapping in real-world robotic navigation systems.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Investigate how to attach lightweight, compressed semantic or global descriptor features to these geometry-only Gaussians for visual place recognition without bloating the primitive size or slowing down geometric convergence.

### 2. G-PROBE: Cross-FOV Place Recognition and Certainty-Coupled Localization for 3D Point Clouds

- **Score:** 4.85/5
- **Evidence level:** `pdf`
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06782

#### The Problem
Global localization from 3D point clouds fails under limited or asymmetric fields of view (FOV). Standard place recognition methods assume dense, symmetric coverage, causing them to collapse when matching narrow-FOV sensors against panoramic maps or across different sensor modalities.

#### The Solution
G-PROBE is a learning-free global localization framework that uses virtual sensor decomposition and certainty-coupled registration (CG-GICP) to handle asymmetric and limited FOVs without requiring dense, symmetric coverage.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** 3D point clouds (narrow, solid-state, or panoramic) $\rightarrow$ 6-DoF metric pose.
- **Backbone or representation:** Virtual sensor decomposition and cross-FOV branch ensembles.
- **Core modules:** Gamma-SGRT for heading-invariant place recognition, and CG-GICP for refinement using a bird's-eye-view (BEV) certainty map.
- **Training or optimization:** Learning-free and tuning-free; relies on geometric and statistical properties of the point cloud.
- **Inference or deployment:** Real-time global localization and registration, robust to sensor differences.
- **Important design choices:** Certainty coupling links the front-end descriptor evaluation directly to the back-end metric pose estimation via a BEV certainty map, eliminating the need for an external verification module.

#### Results
Evaluated on five LiDAR datasets across three modalities, G-PROBE attains the highest learning-free multi-session F1 on average. Under extreme FOV asymmetry (360° to 60°), it retains ~54% Recall@1, which is ~18x higher than the strongest learning-free baseline.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Virtual sensor decomposition effectively normalizes varying FOVs, allowing the same pipeline to run on narrow or panoramic sensors.
- **Finding 2:** BEV certainty maps generated from the front-end descriptor drastically improve back-end registration by suppressing aliased or uncertain regions.
- **Finding 3:** Learning-free methods can outperform zero-shot supervised baselines in cross-sensor and cross-FOV setups.

#### Strengths
Extremely robust to sensor differences and FOV limitations. The certainty-coupled registration is a brilliant, reusable idea for multi-sensor SLAM and global localization.

#### Limitations
Being learning-free, it might struggle with extreme perceptual aliasing or severe weather conditions where learned, context-aware descriptors typically excel.

#### Relevance To My Research
Perfect for global localization, loop closure detection, and multi-sensor calibration. This is a **method to reproduce** and integrate into multi-robot or multi-sensor SLAM pipelines.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Test the BEV certainty map concept as a spatial weighting mechanism for learned 3D descriptors in a deep learning-based place recognition pipeline to improve robustness against dynamic objects and occlusions.

### 3. RoboSnap: One-Shot Real-to-Sim Scene Generation for Generalizable Robot Learning and Evaluation

- **Score:** 4.75/5
- **Evidence level:** `pdf`
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06699

#### The Problem
Constructing physically stable and visually faithful simulation environments from real-world data is slow and expensive. Monolithic NeRF or 3DGS reconstructions often lack the physical collision properties required for interactive robot learning and policy evaluation.

#### The Solution
RoboSnap is a real-to-sim framework that converts a single RGB image into a simulation-ready scene using a layered design: collision-aware foreground assets for stable interaction, and a 3D Gaussian splatting background for faithful visual context.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Single RGB image $\rightarrow$ interactive simulation scene (physical mesh foreground + 3DGS background).
- **Backbone or representation:** 3D Gaussian Splatting for the background; physical meshes for the foreground.
- **Core modules:** Layered scene separation, collision-aware asset refinement, and 3DGS background optimization.
- **Training or optimization:** 3DGS optimization for the background; mesh extraction and refinement for the foreground interaction zones.
- **Inference or deployment:** Used offline to generate synthetic data or evaluate policies in a physics simulator.
- **Important design choices:** Separates physics-critical interaction areas from the visual context, solving the trade-off between physical stability and visual fidelity.

#### Results
Achieves reliable trajectory replay, supports task-specific synthetic data generation, and yields meaningful sim-real correlation for policy evaluation. Introduces DROID-Sim, a companion dataset of 564 real-world scenes.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Layered real-to-sim is significantly more effective than monolithic implicit/explicit representations for interactive robotic tasks.
- **Finding 2:** Single-image scene generation, when properly layered, can yield meaningful sim-real correlation for policy evaluation.
- **Finding 3:** Reusable simulation infrastructure is more valuable for robot learning than purely high-fidelity visual reconstruction.

#### Strengths
Highly practical for building world models and reusable simulation infrastructure. The layered approach elegantly solves the physics-vs-visuals trade-off inherent in 3DGS/NeRF.

#### Limitations
Relying on a single RGB image might lack sufficient geometric detail for complex foreground interactions; it likely depends heavily on depth priors or monocular depth estimation accuracy.

#### Relevance To My Research
Highly relevant to 3D reconstruction, 3DGS, and robot deployment. A **source of experimental ideas** for building sim environments from real-world captures for VLN and manipulation.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Extend the layered approach to use multi-view or feed-forward 3D reconstruction for the foreground to improve the geometric accuracy of interaction zones without sacrificing the 3DGS background.

### 4. NoDrift3R: Raymap-Guided Coupling for Drift-Robust Unposed Feed-Forward 3D Reconstruction

- **Score:** 4.55/5
- **Evidence level:** `pdf`
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07168

#### The Problem
Pose-free feed-forward 3D Gaussian Splatting degrades significantly in long image sequences due to cumulative camera pose estimation drift. This drift propagates errors into geometric modeling and severely limits rendering fidelity and map consistency.

#### The Solution
NoDrift3R is a synergistic pose-free framework that explicitly couples geometry and appearance via a Raymap-Guided Coupling (RGC) module and uses a Dual-Frequency Viewpoint Scheduling strategy to stabilize learning across wide temporal ranges.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Long, unposed image sequences $\rightarrow$ 3DGS map and refined camera poses.
- **Backbone or representation:** Feed-forward 3DGS with raymap-induced geometry anchoring.
- **Core modules:** Raymap-Guided Coupling (RGC) module, Dual-Frequency Viewpoint Scheduling.
- **Training or optimization:** Jointly optimizes RGB reconstruction, raymap consistency, and camera regularization under a unified objective.
- **Inference or deployment:** Feed-forward inference on long sequences, mitigating drift without test-time optimization.
- **Important design choices:** Creates a bidirectional feedback loop where stronger geometry improves rendering, and appearance supervision refines geometry and pose.

#### Results
Shows consistent gains in both rendering and pose estimation across in-domain and cross-domain datasets, with notably improved robustness on long sequences compared to existing pose-free baselines.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Pose drift is the primary bottleneck restricting reconstruction quality in long-sequence feed-forward 3DGS.
- **Finding 2:** Explicit geometry-appearance synergy (via raymaps) stabilizes optimization and prevents local minima.
- **Finding 3:** Dual-Frequency Viewpoint Scheduling (easy-to-hard interval expansion with replay) is crucial for stabilizing learning across varying visual overlaps.

#### Strengths
Directly tackles the scalability of feed-forward 3D reconstruction. The raymap coupling and viewpoint scheduling are strong architectural insights for unposed mapping.

#### Limitations
Feed-forward methods still struggle with extreme viewpoint changes, textureless regions, or severe occlusions compared to optimization-based SLAM systems.

#### Relevance To My Research
Core to feed-forward 3D reconstruction and 3DGS. A **paper to read deeply** for insights on stabilizing unposed, long-sequence reconstruction for scalable mapping.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Integrate the Dual-Frequency Viewpoint Scheduling strategy into our own feed-forward reconstruction training pipeline to improve long-sequence robustness and reduce pose drift.

### 5. EvoPlan: Evolutionary Neuro-Symbolic Robot Planning with Spatio-Temporal Guarantees

- **Score:** 4.55/5
- **Evidence level:** `pdf`
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06724

#### The Problem
LLM-based robot planners are fluent but cannot guarantee that their plans are executable or safe. Classical PDDL planners guarantee these properties but struggle with context reading, open-world vocabulary, and plan repair.

#### The Solution
A neuro-symbolic framework that mines global Signal Temporal Logic (STL) mobility constraints from demonstration data to shield VLM policies, and uses an evolutionary PDDL planner for robust, context-aware task planning.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Demonstration data, VLM policy outputs $\rightarrow$ safe, executable waypoints and plans.
- **Backbone or representation:** Locally-hosted open-weight LLM, Signal Temporal Logic (STL), PDDL.
- **Core modules:** STL constraint miner (using counterfactuals), evolutionary PDDL planner, constrained execution loop.
- **Training or optimization:** Evolutionary search to fit STL constraints from one-class demo data; LLM proposes and repairs PDDL plans.
- **Inference or deployment:** On-robot deployment with no cloud dependency; shields VLM policies in real-time.
- **Important design choices:** Uses LLM for proposal/repair and programmatic validators for survival; shields VLM policies with mined STL rules rather than relying on the LLM for safety.

#### Results
Beats strong baselines on the ALFWorld Text benchmark. Successfully shields a vision-language driving policy on Bench2Drive and discrete-action navigation policies on HA-VLN-CE.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** STL constraints can be effectively mined from one-class demo data using LLM-generated counterfactuals.
- **Finding 2:** Evolutionary PDDL repair is highly robust to vocabulary mismatches between goals and action models.
- **Finding 3:** Local open-weight LLMs are sufficient for on-robot neuro-symbolic planning, eliminating cloud dependency.

#### Strengths
Excellent for safe, reliable VLN and autonomous driving deployment. The STL shielding mechanism is highly actionable for deploying rule-compliant VLM policies in the real world.

#### Limitations
Mining STL constraints might be computationally heavy offline; the quality of the shield is strictly bounded by the quality and diversity of the initial demonstration data.

#### Relevance To My Research
Highly relevant to VLN, autonomous driving, and safe robot navigation. A **source of experimental ideas** for deploying reliable, rule-compliant vision-language policies.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Apply the STL constraint mining technique to our own VLN or driving demonstration datasets to create formal safety shields for our vision-language navigation policies.

### 6. CILC: Cryptographically-secure Inter-agent Loop Closure Candidate Detection for Multi-Agent Collaborative SLAM

- **Score:** 4.5/5
- **Evidence level:** `pdf`
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06700

#### The Problem
In multi-agent SLAM, exchanging global descriptors for loop closure leaks sensitive imagery and trajectory data. Encrypted radios protect against external eavesdroppers, but offer no protection if a swarm member is compromised.

#### The Solution
CILC is a first-of-its-kind system leveraging Secure Multi-Party Computation (SMPC) to detect inter-agent loop closures without exchanging global descriptors in the clear, mitigating information leakage to compromised agents.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Encrypted/secret-shared global descriptors $\rightarrow$ loop closure candidates.
- **Backbone or representation:** Secure Multi-Party Computation (SMPC) protocols.
- **Core modules:** SMPC-based global descriptor similarity comparison.
- **Training or optimization:** N/A (cryptographic protocol design).
- **Inference or deployment:** Real-time, communication-feasible inter-agent loop closure detection.
- **Important design choices:** Applies SMPC only to the lightweight candidate detection step (GD similarity), rather than securing the entire CSLAM pipeline, yielding a favorable privacy-to-overhead trade-off.

#### Results
Validated in both simulation and hardware experiments. Remains real-time and communication-feasible across multimodal GDs (visual and LiDAR), while successfully mitigating information leakage.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Public global descriptors can be reverse-engineered by compromised agents to reconstruct approximations of honest agents' imagery and trajectories.
- **Finding 2:** SMPC can be applied selectively to SLAM front-ends for a highly advantageous privacy-overhead trade-off.
- **Finding 3:** Cryptographic loop closure is feasible in real-time on physical robot hardware.

#### Strengths
Highly novel intersection of cryptography and multi-robot SLAM. Crucial for secure, robust multi-robot deployment in adversarial, military, or privacy-sensitive environments.

#### Limitations
Adds communication and compute overhead; might not scale to massive swarms (e.g., >10 agents) without highly optimized, next-generation SMPC protocols.

#### Relevance To My Research
Directly addresses loop closure detection and multi-agent SLAM. A **paper to read deeply** for secure multi-robot system design and robust deployment.

#### Actionable Follow-Up

### 7. PUF: Plug-and-Play Uncertainty-Aware Fusion for Online 3D Scene Graph Generation

- **Score:** 4.5/5
- **Evidence level:** `pdf`
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07170

#### The Problem
Online 3D scene graph generation typically treats the fusion of 2D observations into a 3D map as a fully deterministic pipeline, ignoring critical sources of uncertainty from the observation, the 2D model, and the 3D representation.

#### The Solution
PUF is a training-free, uncertainty-aware fusion framework that formulates scene graph node association probabilistically and uses Dirichlet evidence accumulation to distribute class and relationship evidence across plausible candidates.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** 2D observations, 3D map (Gaussian or voxel) $\rightarrow$ online 3D scene graph.
- **Backbone or representation:** 3D Gaussian Splatting or 3D voxel backend.
- **Core modules:** Probabilistic likelihood association, Dirichlet evidence accumulation, class-conditional prior for sparse pairs.
- **Training or optimization:** Training-free; relies on probabilistic fusion rules.
- **Inference or deployment:** Real-time online fusion during robot navigation.
- **Important design choices:** Replaces binary accept/reject gates with probabilistic likelihoods; designed to be backend-agnostic.

#### Results
Instantiated with both 3D Gaussian and voxel backends, achieving SOTA on 3DSSG and ReplicaSSG benchmarks while maintaining real-time latency.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Modeling uncertainty in 2D-to-3D fusion significantly improves scene graph accuracy and reduces false associations.
- **Finding 2:** Dirichlet evidence accumulation effectively handles sparse or conflicting observations over time.
- **Finding 3:** The probabilistic fusion framework is backend-agnostic and works seamlessly with 3DGS.

#### Strengths
Highly transferable to VLN memory design and spatial understanding. The probabilistic fusion into 3DGS is directly applicable to building robust semantic maps for navigation.

#### Limitations
Scene graphs might become overly dense or computationally heavy to query in very large, cluttered environments if pruning strategies are not applied.

#### Relevance To My Research
Aligns perfectly with 3D scene understanding, spatial memory, and 3DGS. A **method to reproduce** for building robust, uncertainty-aware semantic maps.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Integrate PUF's Dirichlet evidence accumulation into our 3DGS-based spatial memory module to improve semantic consistency and handle observation uncertainty in VLN.

### 8. Validate the Dream Before You Trust Its Verdict: Admissibility for World-Model Simulators

- **Score:** 4.35/5
- **Evidence level:** `pdf`
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07196

#### The Problem
Generative World Models used as closed-loop simulators are often evaluated on visual fidelity metrics (e.g., FVD), which ignore whether the world responds correctly to the policy's actions. Visual realism does not guarantee action-robustness.

#### The Solution
The paper defines an admissibility ladder (L0-L4) for certifying generative World Models, adapting Verification, Validation & Accreditation (VV&A) and Safety of the Intended Functionality (SOTIF) frameworks to evaluate when a WM's verdicts can be trusted as assurance evidence.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** World Model, action policies $\rightarrow$ admissibility certification (L0-L4).
- **Backbone or representation:** VV&A and SOTIF safety frameworks adapted for generative AI.
- **Core modules:** Admissibility ladder definitions, action-following metrics, scenario-based testing.
- **Training or optimization:** N/A (evaluation and certification framework).
- **Inference or deployment:** Used offline to validate WMs before deploying them as test oracles or simulators.
- **Important design choices:** Explicitly separates visual generation quality (L0) from action-robustness and physics consistency (L1-L2).

#### Results
Applied to two driving WMs, revealing a critical reversal: the model ranking higher on visual generation quality (L0) ranks lower on action-following (L1-L2), proving visual fidelity does not predict closed-loop reliability.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Visual fidelity metrics like FVD are insufficient and potentially misleading for certifying closed-loop simulators.
- **Finding 2:** Action-following robustness must be explicitly tested (L1-L2) to ensure the simulator responds correctly to control inputs.
- **Finding 3:** Generative WMs need formal accreditation before their closed-loop verdicts can serve as assurance evidence for safety-critical systems.

#### Strengths
Provides a crucial conceptual framework for evaluating world models in autonomous driving. Highly inspiring for designing reliable, certifiable real-world AD systems.

#### Limitations
Theoretical and framework-heavy; lacks a concrete, automated software toolchain for applying the L0-L4 ladder to arbitrary WMs out-of-the-box.

#### Relevance To My Research
Directly addresses world models and robust autonomous driving deployment. A **paper to read deeply** for evaluation methodology and safety certification.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Develop a concrete set of action-robustness benchmarks (L1-L2) based on this paper's framework to systematically evaluate our own driving world models and video generators.

### 9. PLED-VINS: A Point-Line Event-Based Visual Inertial SLAM for Dynamic Environments

- **Score:** 4.25/5
- **Evidence level:** `pdf`
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07374

#### The Problem
Dynamic environments degrade visual SLAM accuracy. While event cameras preserve fine-grained spatio-temporal information, most event-based SLAM frameworks assume static scenes and lack mechanisms to estimate the temporal reliability of features without heavy semantic segmentation.

#### The Solution
PLED-VINS is a monocular event-based VIO SLAM framework that uses an entropy-recency score map to evaluate the temporal reliability of point and line features based on event statistics, combined with geometric reliability in bundle adjustment.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Event camera streams, IMU data $\rightarrow$ camera poses, 3D map.
- **Backbone or representation:** Event-based point and line features.
- **Core modules:** Entropy-recency score map, unified point-line robust bundle adjustment, adaptive weighting strategy.
- **Training or optimization:** Optimization-based SLAM (Bundle Adjustment).
- **Inference or deployment:** Real-time state estimation on edge hardware.
- **Important design choices:** Uses event temporal statistics to suppress dynamic objects and unreliable features without relying on computationally heavy neural segmentation networks.

#### Results
Experimental results demonstrate that PLED-VINS significantly improves state estimation accuracy on dynamic sequences with moving objects compared to static-assumption baselines.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Event temporal statistics (entropy and recency) can effectively characterize feature reliability and identify dynamic outliers.
- **Finding 2:** Combining temporal reliability with geometric reliability in BA robustly suppresses dynamic observations.
- **Finding 3:** Point-line fusion in event SLAM improves structural robustness in dynamic, texture-degraded scenes.

#### Strengths
Lightweight, deployable alternative to semantic segmentation for dynamic SLAM. Highly relevant for multi-sensor (event+IMU) state estimation in real-world deployment.

#### Limitations
Event cameras still struggle in low-texture or purely static scenes with slow motion; line feature extraction from events can be noisy in highly cluttered environments.

#### Relevance To My Research
Addresses dynamic SLAM and multi-sensor perception. A **source of experimental ideas** for robust localization in dynamic environments without heavy compute.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Explore adapting the entropy-recency score map concept for standard RGB-D or LiDAR SLAM to reject dynamic objects and unstable features without relying on heavy neural segmentation networks.

### 10. Disturbance-aware Motion Planning for Over-actuated Underwater Vehicles Exploiting Actuation Redundancy for High-fidelity 3D Reconstruction

- **Score:** 3.9/5
- **Evidence level:** `pdf`
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07139

#### The Problem
Underwater robots operating near delicate targets resuspend sediments and induce turbulence with their high-power thrusters, severely degrading image quality and ruining downstream 3D reconstruction.

#### The Solution
The paper exploits actuation redundancy in over-actuated ROVs (8 thrusters) to minimize thruster-induced hydrodynamic disturbance in the target region while maintaining desired motion constraints, using a real-time redundancy-resolving allocator.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Desired vehicle motion, target region $\rightarrow$ optimized thruster allocation.
- **Backbone or representation:** Control-oriented thruster-wake proxy derived from actuator-disk theory.
- **Core modules:** Null-space search for redundancy resolution, real-time allocator (10 Hz).
- **Training or optimization:** Optimization-based control allocation minimizing predicted disturbance.
- **Inference or deployment:** Real-time autonomous scanning or operator-assisted inspection.
- **Important design choices:** Perception-aware control that explicitly minimizes physical disturbance in the sensor's field of view to preserve scene integrity.

#### Results
Reduces target-region particle velocity by 67%, improves 3D reconstruction RMSE by 55% (1.9 mm vs 4.3 mm), and achieves a 98.5% reconstruction success rate across 440 trials.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Actuation-to-perception coupling is critical in fluid or degraded environments; standard controllers ignore this to the detriment of sensing.
- **Finding 2:** Exploiting null-space redundancy in over-actuated platforms can significantly improve downstream perception without altering the vehicle's trajectory.
- **Finding 3:** Simple analytical wake proxies are sufficient for real-time perception-aware control.

#### Strengths
The concept of perception-aware control to preserve scene integrity is highly inspiring for autonomous systems operating in sensitive or degraded environments (e.g., dust, snow, underwater).

#### Limitations
Highly domain-specific to underwater ROVs and fluid dynamics; direct mathematical transfer to terrestrial wheeled or legged robots is limited.

#### Relevance To My Research
Relevant to robust robot deployment and 3D reconstruction pipelines. A **source of conceptual inspiration** for perception-aware planning in degraded visual environments.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Investigate how perception-aware control (e.g., minimizing dust kick-up, avoiding self-occlusion, or managing sensor vibration) can be formulated for terrestrial legged or wheeled robots operating in degraded visual environments.

## Trends Of The Day

- **Geometry-First 3D Representations:** A strong shift towards prioritizing geometric accuracy over photorealism in 3DGS and SLAM (GeoGS-SLAM, NoDrift3R), recognizing that downstream robotics tasks require reliable spatial structures rather than just novel view synthesis.
- **Robustness in Adverse/Asymmetric Conditions:** Significant focus on handling real-world sensor and environmental limitations, such as asymmetric FOVs in LiDAR (G-PROBE), dynamic environments in event cameras (PLED-VINS), and fluid disturbances in underwater ROVs.

- **Layered and Probabilistic Scene Understanding:** Moving away from monolithic deterministic pipelines towards layered real-to-sim environments (RoboSnap) and uncertainty-aware semantic fusion (PUF) to handle the messy reality of real-world data.
