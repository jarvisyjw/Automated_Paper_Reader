### 1. World Model Self-Distillation: Training World Models to Solve General Tasks

- **Score:** 4.30/5
- **Evidence level:** `pdf`
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12072

#### The Problem
Pretrained video generators (world models) exhibit emergent task-solving abilities but rely on detailed textual descriptions, limiting their direct use for autonomous planning and decision-making in robotics. Collecting paired task-execution videos for supervised fine-tuning is costly and hard to scale.

#### The Solution
The paper proposes World Model Self-Distillation (WMSD), a framework that combines self-distillation with reinforcement learning. It distills a caption-conditioned video diffusion model (Demonstrator) into an instruction-conditioned model (Executor), and further refines the Executor using VLM-based reward feedback to solve general tasks without curated task-video supervision.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Initial scene image and a short task instruction -> generated video trajectory.
- **Backbone or representation:** Conditional flow-matching video generators.
- **Core modules:** Demonstrator (teacher), Executor (student), and a VLM-based reward model.
- **Training or optimization:** Off-policy and on-policy distillation losses combined with an RL objective that uses VLM task rewards and distillation rewards.
- **Inference or deployment:** The Executor generates task-solving video rollouts conditioned only on the image and short prompt, which can be used for planning.
- **Important design choices:** Exploits the generation-verification asymmetry by using a VLM as a verifier rather than a generator for RL rewards.

#### Results
Evaluated on the proposed WorldTasks-Bench and the DreamGen robotics benchmark. The Executor surpasses the Demonstrator under the VLM-based evaluation protocol and transfers competitively to robotic tasks compared to methods trained with curated supervision.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Self-distillation effectively transfers execution knowledge from detailed captions to short instructions.
- **Finding 2:** VLM feedback combined with distillation regularization stabilizes RL for multi-step video generators.
- **Finding 3:** The Executor can surpass its teacher by optimizing for task success rather than just imitating the teacher's distribution.

#### Strengths
Highly relevant to world models and robot planning. The combination of distillation and RL addresses the computational bottleneck of applying RL to diffusion models. The approach is scalable and avoids the need for expensive paired task-video datasets.

#### Limitations
VLM rewards can be noisy and inconsistent for ambiguous visual tasks. The method relies heavily on the quality of the pretrained video generator and the VLM's ability to accurately verify task completion.

#### Relevance To My Research
Directly connects to my interest in world models and robot navigation. It provides a strong foundation for building instruction-following world models that can be used for spatial memory generation and vision-language navigation planning. A paper to read deeply for world-modeling insights.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Investigate applying the WMSD framework to 3D-aware world models or spatial memory representations for VLN, using a 3D VLM as the verifier to ensure geometric consistency in generated trajectories.

### 2. Ambient Diffusion Policy: Imitation Learning from Suboptimal Data in Robotics

- **Score:** 4.20/5
- **Evidence level:** `pdf`
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12365

#### The Problem
High-quality, task-specific robot data is expensive to collect, while suboptimal or out-of-distribution datasets are abundant. Naively co-training on mixed-quality data biases the policy and degrades performance, limiting robust robot deployment.

#### The Solution
Ambient Diffusion Policy introduces noise-dependent data usage. By exploiting the spectral power law of robot action data, it restricts the contribution of suboptimal data to only high and low diffusion times, allowing the policy to learn useful global features while ignoring harmful local suboptimality.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Visual/proprioceptive observations -> action chunks.
- **Backbone or representation:** Diffusion Policy.
- **Core modules:** A modified data sampler that filters suboptimal data based on diffusion time.
- **Training or optimization:** Suboptimal data is only used at specific high and low noise intervals, while high-quality data is used across all noise levels.
- **Inference or deployment:** Standard reverse diffusion sampling from the trained policy.
- **Important design choices:** Leverages the global-to-local hierarchy induced by the spectral power law, where high noise corresponds to global planning and low noise to local motion primitives.

#### Results
Validated on four types of suboptimal data (noisy, sim-to-real, task mismatch, large-scale mixtures) across six tasks. Outperforms co-training baselines by up to 33% on Open X-Embodiment. Continues to improve as suboptimal data scales, whereas baselines plateau.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Robot action data exhibits a spectral power law, inducing a global-to-local hierarchy in diffusion models.
- **Finding 2:** Gaussian noise acts as a high-frequency mask, erasing local suboptimality at high diffusion times.
- **Finding 3:** Restricting suboptimal data to specific noise levels is more effective than data filtering or simple loss re-weighting.

#### Strengths
Extremely simple to implement (requires only a single change to the data sampler) yet highly effective. The theoretical grounding via the spectral power law provides strong intuition. Highly valuable for robust robot deployment using heterogeneous datasets.

#### Limitations
Assumes the spectral power law holds universally across all robot embodiments and action representations. May not generalize well to tasks where high-frequency details are critical and differ significantly between target and suboptimal distributions.

#### Relevance To My Research
Highly relevant for robust robot deployment and learning from diverse, noisy datasets. The concept of noise-dependent data usage could be transferred to training 3D reconstruction or VLN memory models from mixed-quality data. A method to reproduce and adapt.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Apply noise-dependent data sampling to feed-forward 3D reconstruction datasets with mixed quality (e.g., varying baseline lengths or noisy poses) to see if global geometry can be learned from noisy data while preserving local detail from high-quality data.

### 3. Learning Unions of Convex Sets via Invertible Latent Decomposition for Path Planning

- **Score:** 3.90/5
- **Evidence level:** `pdf`
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12027

#### The Problem
Explicit representations (unions of convex sets) provide hard collision-free guarantees for optimization-based planners but scale poorly with configuration-space dimension. Implicit representations scale well but lack explicit constraints for safe planning.

#### The Solution
Invertible Latent Decomposition (ILD) jointly learns an invertible neural network mapping and a union of explicit convex polytopes in the latent space. Planning is performed in the latent space and decoded back to the configuration space, preserving feasibility.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Robot configuration space -> Latent convex polytopes -> Collision-free paths.
- **Backbone or representation:** Invertible Neural Networks (LU decomposition), Graph of Convex Sets (GCS).
- **Core modules:** Invertible latent mapping, Visibility-Guided Sampling (VGS) for connectivity, test-time refinement.
- **Training or optimization:** Jointly optimizes the mapping and hyperplane parameters using BCE loss, geometric losses, and VGS.
- **Inference or deployment:** Plans over latent convex sets using GCS, decodes paths via the invertible mapping. Test-time refinement corrects false positives.
- **Important design choices:** Uses invertibility to guarantee a one-to-one correspondence between latent and configuration spaces, ensuring decoded paths remain collision-free.

#### Results
Evaluated on 2D navigation, 6-DoF, and 14-DoF manipulation. Achieves broader coverage and higher success rates than baselines. Demonstrated real-time collision-free planning on a 14-DoF bimanual manipulator with zero false positives after test-time refinement.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Invertible mappings can warp complex, non-convex configuration spaces into compact latent spaces suitable for convex decomposition.
- **Finding 2:** Visibility-Guided Sampling significantly improves inter-region connectivity for optimization-based planners.
- **Finding 3:** Test-time refinement of explicit boundaries effectively eliminates false positives without retraining.

#### Strengths
Bridges the gap between explicit and implicit representations. Strong real-world deployment on high-DoF systems. The test-time refinement mechanism is highly practical for safe robot deployment.

#### Limitations
Invertible networks may struggle with highly complex, non-homeomorphic configuration spaces or environments with extremely narrow passages. The computational cost of training the invertible mapping in very high dimensions is not fully explored.

#### Relevance To My Research
Relevant to robot navigation, explicit/implicit 3D representations, and safe deployment. The idea of using invertible mappings to simplify complex geometric spaces could inspire new approaches to 3D scene representation and spatial memory design. A paper to read deeply for geometric planning insights.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Explore combining ILD with 3D Gaussian or implicit scene representations to create invertible latent spaces for safe, optimization-based visual-language navigation in complex 3D environments.

### 4. Blind Dexterous Grasping via Real2Sim2Real Tactile Policy Learning

- **Score:** 3.75/5
- **Evidence level:** `pdf`
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11767

#### The Problem
Learning tactile-only blind grasping policies for real robots is hindered by the tactile sim-to-real gap (especially in contact timing) and the limited expressiveness of sparse binary tactile signals.

#### The Solution
A Real2Sim2Real framework featuring a lightweight contact-event calibration pipeline, a layout-aware tactile encoder pretrained with privileged geometric supervision, and an expert-to-diffusion policy distillation scheme.

#### How It Works
- **Inputs and outputs:** Proprioception + binary tactile signals -> Dexterous hand joint actions.
- **Backbone or representation:** Diffusion Policy, Temporal Transformer encoder.
- **Core modules:** Real2Sim tactile calibration, layout-aware pretraining, PPO experts for data generation.
- **Training or optimization:** Calibrates sim contact parameters -> trains PPO experts -> pretrains encoder with privileged info -> distills to Diffusion Policy.
- **Inference or deployment:** Zero-shot sim-to-real deployment on a physical LEAP Hand without visual input.
- **Important design choices:** Uses binary contact events and calibrates their onset/offset timing to minimize the sim-to-real gap, avoiding complex soft-body modeling.

#### Results
Evaluated on a physical LEAP Hand across 20 objects (10 seen, 10 unseen). Achieves a 27% real-world grasp success rate without real-world demonstrations or vision. Ablations confirm the value of layout-aware pretraining and contact calibration.

#### Key Findings
- **Finding 1:** Binary tactile signals still exhibit a sim-to-real gap in contact timing, which can be mitigated via lightweight parameter calibration.
- **Finding 2:** Injecting 3D sensor layout priors and privileged geometric supervision improves reasoning from sparse tactile observations.
- **Finding 3:** Distilling object-specific RL experts into a unified Diffusion Policy handles multimodal grasping strategies under partial observability.

#### Strengths
Highly practical sim-to-real calibration pipeline. The layout-aware encoder is a clever way to add spatial context to sparse sensors. Demonstrates robust deployment on physical hardware.

#### Limitations
The 27% success rate, while notable for blind grasping, is still relatively low for industrial applications. Binary tactile feedback discards rich force and shear information that could improve stability.

#### Relevance To My Research
Connects to multi-sensor calibration, sim-to-real transfer, and robust robot deployment. The Real2Sim calibration approach for temporal/spatial alignment is highly transferable to multi-sensor perception in autonomous driving. A source of experimental ideas for calibration.

#### Actionable Follow-Up
Adapt the Real2Sim contact calibration pipeline (minimizing L1 mismatch between real and simulated event sequences) for multi-sensor (e.g., LiDAR-camera) temporal and spatial alignment in autonomous driving perception systems.

## Trends Of The Day

- **Diffusion Policies for Robotics:** Diffusion models continue to dominate robot learning, with novel adaptations for suboptimal data (Ambient Diffusion) and multimodal tactile grasping. This trend looks mature but is still yielding highly practical deployment strategies.
- **World Models and VLM Integration:** Using VLMs as verifiers or reward models for world models (WMSD) is an emerging and highly promising direction, bridging the gap between generative video models and actionable robot planning.
- **Sim-to-Real Calibration:** There is a strong focus on lightweight, practical calibration methods (e.g., binary contact timing, invertible latent spaces) rather than attempting perfect physical simulation, indicating a mature, deployment-first mindset in robotics research.

## Potential Research Ideas

- **Noise-Dependent 3D Reconstruction:** Apply the spectral power law and noise-dependent data sampling from Ambient Diffusion to feed-forward 3D reconstruction, using noisy/uncalibrated data for global scene layout and high-quality data for local geometry.
- **3D-Aware World Model Verification:** Extend WMSD by replacing the 2D VLM verifier with a 3D VLM or geometric consistency checker, ensuring that generated world model trajectories respect 3D spatial memory and physics.
- **Invertible Latent Spatial Memory:** Use invertible neural networks to map complex, non-convex 3D environments (represented by 3D Gaussians) into a simplified latent space for efficient, optimization-based vision-language navigation.

## Papers Worth Adding To Related Work
